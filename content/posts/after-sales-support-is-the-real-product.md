I recently bought a ceiling fan from Kuhl which came with "free installation".

The experience was simple:

- Wait 4 days  
- Nobody comes
- No installation 
- No accountability

Ultimately I returned the fan - it took around 15 minutes over a phone call
with Amazon's customer care to initiate the return process. This time could
have been better spent on something else...

Now yes - I *can* install a ceiling fan myself.

That is not the point.

I did not pay money to become an unpaid field technician coordinating support
logistics for a hardware company.

In contrast, Atomberg's service has been consistently prompt and professional
for me across multiple Pune locations - Hadapsar, Undri and Manjri. YMMV
though!

That matters.

Before buying the product based on Amazon "Reviews", I should have looked at
the Google Play Store reviews! The `Kuhl Fan` app is very poorly rated for good
reasons...

![Kuhl Crap 1](/images/Kuhl-Crap-1.jpg)

![Kuhl Crap 2](/images/Kuhl-Crap-2.jpg)

A ceiling fan is not a SaaS product where you can ship broken experiences and
patch them later with emails and apologies.

Physical appliance products require physical execution:

- Installation networks
- Local coordination
- Spare availability
- Warranty handling
- Actual humans showing up

Indian consumers are slowly realizing something important:

A slightly less fancy product with reliable service is often better than a
"premium" product with startup-style operational chaos.

Especially for appliances.

Specs are temporary.  

After-sales support is the real product.
